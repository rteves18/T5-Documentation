IVR Menus

by Ryan Teves

Posted on April 30, 2017 at 9:00 PM

What is an IVR Menu?

IVR stands for Interactive Voice Response. In short, it allows customers to specify their needs so that they may be redirected to the appropriate caller representative. We’ve all encountered IVR Menus once in awhile even if we haven’t realize it. When we call our Bank, customer support, etc. When a customer calls a company, the company’s phone line would need to know the reason why the customer is calling to ensure that customers are connected to the appropriate associate. Had this process be done manually, surely it would take a long time for each customer to get connected to the appropriate department that will best suit their needs, especially when there are high volumes of callers. IVR menus simply automate this process.

Typically, a customer will be prompted to specify the reason why they are calling, either by using the phone number pad or voice interaction. Once a customer has specified their need, the IVR menu can be set up in several ways. One could be that the customer is placed in a queue and will be put on hold until the next available representative within a specific department is able to take their call. These groups of representatives within a specific department are called Ring Groups, which can also be set up. If the need of a customer can be handled by a particular individual, the IVR menu can redirect the call to any individual as necessary. If the customer is calling for a frequently asked question or calling during after hours, automated prompts can also be setup to ensure the customer gets the exact information he or she is looking for.

Ideas in setting up an IVR Menu?

Usually, a company consist of several departments and the IVR menu can be broken as such; whether that be sales, customer service, tech support, etc. When it comes to setting up an IVR menu, it is in everyone’s interest to keeping things simple to ensure a seamlessly easy experience for the customer. Or as Peter Massey from Budd UK says, “Keep it simple. The theory goes that you can remember 7 bits of information, so don’t say much in any menu offering.” For more tips to improve your IVR menu, click here.

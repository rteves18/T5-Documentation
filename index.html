IVR Menus

by Ryan Teves

Posted on May 28, 2017 at 9:00 PM

What is an IVR Menu?

IVR stands for Interactive Voice Response. In short, it allows customers to specify their needs so that they may be redirected to the appropriate caller representative or department. For a more technical definition and explanation of what an IVR Menu is, a good place to look is here and here. Basically and IVR can be thought of as an Auto Attendant, after-hours MENU, or voice prompt system. To get started in creating your very own IVR menu(s) lets look at some examples.

IVR Menu Example

For this example let us imagine a customer is calling an Auto-Repair Shop. When a customer calls during hours of operation a typically IVR menu prompt would sound as follows:

Thank you for calling Bob’s Auto-Repair Shop:
Let us know how we could be of service!
For general maintenance, press 1
For parts, press 2
For home visits, press 3
For other services and inquires, press 4

Once a customer has picked an option that best suits their needs, one of three things could happen. Either the customer can then be redirected to an individual representative, placed into a queue until the next representative is available in the case of high volume calls, or prompted with yet another IVR menu that will request the customer to further specify their needs and the same process happens all over again.

And when a customer calls after hours of operations, a typically IVR menu prompt would sound along the lines of:

Thank you for calling Bob’s Auto-Repair Shop:
Unfortunately we are currently closed.
Our hours of operation are Monday through Saturday, from 9am to 6pm
To leave a voicemail, please stay on the line
If situation is urgent, please press 3

Benefits to setting IVR Menus

As mentioned before, IVR menus can be thought of as Auto Attendant, after-hours menu, or voice prompt system. Meaning, whether a customer calls during hours of operations or after, each and every customer’s call can conveniently and efficiently handled and redirected depending on the hour of the day. This rules out the need for a receptionist to manually redirect every incoming call to the appropriate department, which can become overwhelming especially when there are a high volume of callers.

Ideas in setting up an IVR Menu?

Usually, a company consist of several departments and the IVR menu can be broken as such; whether that be sales, customer service, tech support, etc. When it comes to setting up an IVR menu, it is in everyone’s interest to keeping things simple to ensure a seamlessly easy experience for the customer. Or as Peter Massey from Budd UK says, “Keep it simple. The theory goes that you can remember 7 bits of information, so don’t say much in any menu offering.” For more tips to improve your IVR menu, click here.
